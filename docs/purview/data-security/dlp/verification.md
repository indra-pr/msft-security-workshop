# Data Loss Prevention (DLP) — Part 4

!!! abstract "Step 4 of 4 · Verification"

## 7. Verify it works

Use the [sample data from Part 1](index.md#3-generate-sample-data-for-your-lab) to trigger the policy, then confirm the signal shows up.

### Trigger the policy

=== "Email test"

    1. From a test mailbox, compose an email to an **external** address.
    2. Paste the contents of `payment-memo.txt` (contains a synthetic credit card number) into the body.
    3. If policy tips are on, you should see a **policy tip** warning that the message contains sensitive info. In enforce mode you'd be blocked (with override).

=== "SharePoint / OneDrive test"

    1. Upload `customer-export.csv` to a SharePoint site or OneDrive covered by the policy.
    2. Attempt to **share** it with an external user.
    3. A policy tip / restriction should appear for external sharing.

### Confirm the match was recorded

1. In the **[Microsoft Purview portal](https://purview.microsoft.com)**, open **Data Loss Prevention → Alerts**.
2. Find the alert generated by your test. Open it to see the **matched sensitive information type**, the **user**, the **location**, and the **action** taken.
3. Open **Data Loss Prevention → Overview** and review the **Risk Spotlighting** card and (after ~7 days of activity) **DLP analytics** recommendations.
4. Cross-check activity in **Activity explorer** — filter for **DLPRuleMatch** events.

!!! success "What 'good' looks like"
    - Your test action appears as a **DLP alert** with the correct SIT (Credit Card Number) and severity.
    - In **simulation mode**, you see matches **without** users being blocked.
    - The alert's details show the **rule**, **action**, and (if a user overrode) the **justification**.

!!! note "Timing"
    Policies can take time to fully deploy across all locations. DLP **analytics** recommendations appear about **seven days** after you enable analytics; give simulation a few days of real activity before you tune.

## 8. Extensibility

- **Custom sensitive information types** — define your own patterns (regex + keywords + [document fingerprinting](https://learn.microsoft.com/purview/sit-document-fingerprinting)) when built-in SITs don't match your data.
- **Adaptive Protection** — connect [Insider Risk Management](https://learn.microsoft.com/purview/insider-risk-management-adaptive-protection) so DLP actions tighten automatically for higher-risk users.
- **Microsoft Defender for Cloud Apps** — extend DLP-style controls to third-party SaaS apps and sessions.
- **Power Automate & Graph** — DLP alerts flow into the unified alerts pipeline; use the [Microsoft Graph security API](https://learn.microsoft.com/graph/api/resources/security-api-overview) and Power Automate to route alerts to ticketing/SOAR.
- **Microsoft Security Copilot in Purview** — natural-language investigation of DLP alerts. See [Security Copilot in Purview](https://learn.microsoft.com/purview/copilot-in-purview-overview).

### Integration requirements

| Integration | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Endpoint DLP | Onboarded Windows 10/11 (x64, build 1809+) or macOS devices |
| Chrome coverage | [Microsoft Purview extension for Chrome](https://learn.microsoft.com/purview/dlp-chrome-learn-about) on onboarded devices |
| Adaptive Protection | Insider Risk Management configured; appropriate role groups |
| SOAR/ticketing | Graph security API permissions; Power Automate or a connector |

## 9. Industry use cases

=== "Financial services"

    Block outbound email and external SharePoint sharing that contains **primary account numbers (PAN)** to support **PCI DSS**; use **block with override** so brokers can still service clients with a logged justification.

=== "Telco"

    Prevent leakage of **subscriber PII and CDR (call detail record)** exports to personal cloud storage from agent workstations using **endpoint DLP**.

=== "Public sector & SOE"

    Enforce **data residency and classification** rules — block sharing of citizen records labeled *Highly Confidential* to external or personal accounts; pair DLP with sensitivity labels.

=== "Energy & resources"

    Protect **operational technology diagrams, well/seismic data, and grid schematics** from being emailed to competitors or uploaded to consumer apps.

=== "Manufacturing & conglomerates"

    Stop exfiltration of **CAD files, BOMs, and supplier pricing** across business units; scope rules per business unit using admin units.
